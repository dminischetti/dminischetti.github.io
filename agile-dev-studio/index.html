Instructional Methodology: The Agile Cycle

Figure 1.0: Professional Sprint Workflow for the CTE Classroom
Industry Alignment: I transform the classroom into a simulated dev agency. By following the 5-step Sprint Cycle, students move beyond "finishing an assignment" to "delivering a product," fostering accountability and professional identity (Danielson 3c, 4a).

Meeting the Needs of All Learners

Figure 2.0: Scaffolding Pyramid for Diverse Learners (SWD/ELL)
Equity in CS: My instruction is built on a Tiered Scaffolding model. While Tier 3 students lead architecture decisions, Tier 1 learners are supported with visual logic maps and starter code templates, ensuring 100% participation and IEP compliance (Danielson 1b).

Project Phases

  • Discovery & DesignCheckpoint

    User stories, wireframes, and data mapping. Setting the foundation before the first line of code.

  • Development SprintsCheckpoint

    Guided coding cycles with daily stand-ups to track blockers and progress.

  • Quality Assurance (QA)Checkpoint

    Cross-team peer testing to identify bugs and practice technical feedback.

  • Demo DayFinal

    Professional presentation of the MVP to stakeholders.
