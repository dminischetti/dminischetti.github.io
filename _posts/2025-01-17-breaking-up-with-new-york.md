New York and I have been in a long-term relationship. At first, it was exciting—fast-paced, unpredictable, full of charm. It felt like living in a movie where I was both the protagonist and an unpaid extra at the same time.

But like any relationship, time changes things. At some point, you stop romanticizing the quirks and start realizing that maybe—*just maybe*—this isn’t your forever person. Or, in this case, your forever city.

So here it is, NYC: **I think we need to break up.**

## **1. You Never Let Me Breathe (Literally and Figuratively)**

You know what would be nice? Air. Not the kind that smells like hot garbage in summer or mystery smoke in winter. Not the kind that makes me wonder if my lung capacity is slowly being replaced by car exhaust and halal cart fumes.

I walk everywhere—I *love* walking—but I also love not inhaling a cocktail of pollutants while doing it. And don’t get me started on personal space. **If I can feel a stranger’s breath on my neck while waiting for my bagel, we have a problem.**

## **2. Your Prices Are a Personal Attack**

New York, we need to talk about your spending habits.

- **$7 for a coffee?** Why? Did a monk grind the beans between his wisdom-filled hands?
- **$3,000 for rent?** Is there a secret gold vault hidden in my 500-square-foot apartment?
- **$12 for eggs?** Did the chickens get private tutoring before they laid them?

I’m not saying I want to go full countryside and start milking my own cows, but at this rate, it might be the only financially responsible choice.

## **3. You’re Aging Me at an Alarming Rate**

Every day in this city feels like a mini-boss battle.

- The subway? A real-life RNG experience where the odds are never in your favor.
- The sidewalks? A competitive sport where you must dodge strollers, pigeons, and people who stop in the middle for NO REASON.
- The grocery store? A psychological test in patience and endurance.

I moved here with youthful optimism. Now I look in the mirror and see a guy who just *wants to sit in peace for five minutes without hearing a car alarm.*

## **4. I Just Want a Real Kitchen**

Look, I love food. I love cooking. But cooking in a NYC kitchen is an *exercise in humility*.

- Counter space? A cruel joke.
- Storage? A Tetris puzzle where the prize is knocking over the olive oil every time I open a cabinet.
- The oven? Either a shoebox or a prehistoric artifact that requires a secret chant to ignite properly.

I want to cook a meal without feeling like I’m filming a survival show. Is that too much to ask?

## **5. I Have Kids Now, and You Are… Not Kid-Friendly**

I used to think NYC was “exciting” and “full of energy.” Now I just think it’s **loud and exhausting**—two things that don’t mix well with a baby who needs naps and a 9-year-old who just wants to ride her bike without dodging delivery guys on electric scooters.

Everything takes **ten times more effort** when you have kids here. Want to go to a park? Great, first you have to navigate through crowds, figure out if the slides are currently being used as a skateboard ramp, and hope no one is aggressively feeding the squirrels again.

Want to drive somewhere? *Hah. Cute.*

## **So Where Does That Leave Us?**

New York, I loved you once. Maybe I even still do, in a complicated, nostalgic kind of way.

But I think we want different things. You want high energy, constant movement, and people willing to pay $20 for avocado toast. I want air, space, **a house with an actual laundry room**, and a cost of living that doesn’t make me question my life choices.

It’s time for me to move on. And don’t get me wrong—I’ll visit. I’ll come back for the food, the memories, the occasional chaotic adventure.

But as for living here?

**Yeah… I think I’m ready for a new chapter.**
